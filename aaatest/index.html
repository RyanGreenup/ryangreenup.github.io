        • It's not a live search which makes refining search terms difficult
        • I can't flick through searched items / notes with the keyboard, I have to individually click each item (and the preview takes a few seconds).

    • Email to Zheng Related to Damping

      Dear Dr Zheng,

      Sorry for the late reply, I've been really busy with Christmas.

      • Should \(k\) always be positive?

        I wasn't able to clearly interpret what the Bloom paper was trying to express with regard to any values for damping, it just wasn't clear to me.

        Instead what i did was:

        1. Solve the Cross Product to given the Bloch Equations
        2. Numerically Solve the Bloch Equations
        3. Generate a Plot
        4. Reduce the domain of the plot until the plot has a reasonable number of periods
        5. Create a dynamic Plot allowing me to sweep a range of \(k\) values that produce any change.
        • Laboratory Frame
          • Math

            So in the laboratory frame:

            \[\begin{aligned} \mathbf{B}&= \begin{pmatrix} B_{\mathrm{RF}}\cdot \cos\left( \omega_{\mathrm{RF}}\cdot t + \phi_{0} \right) - k\cdot M_y \\ B_{\mathrm{RF}}\cdot \sin\left( \omega_{\mathrm{RF}}\cdot t+ \phi_0 \right)+ k\cdot M_x \\ B_0 \\ \end{pmatrix} \\ \mathbf{M}&= \begin{pmatrix} M_x \\ M_y \\ M_z\\\end{pmatrix} \\ \end{aligned}\]

            and hence:

            \[\begin{aligned} \frac{\mathrm{d}\mathbf{M} }{\mathrm{d} t}&= M\times B - \mathbf{R}\left( \mathbf{M} - M_0 \right)\\ &= \begin{pmatrix} \gamma\left[ B_0 y - k x z + B_{\mathrm{RF}} z \sin\left( \phi + t \omega \right) \right] - R_2 x \\ \gamma\left[ - B_0 x - k y z + B_{\mathrm{RF}} z \cos \left( \phi + t \omega \right) \right] - R_2 y \\ \gamma\left[ kx^2 + k y^2 - B_{\mathrm{RF}}y \cos\left( \phi + t \omega \right)- B_{\mathrm{RF}} x \sin\left( \phi + t \omega \right) \right] - R_1\left( z- M_0 \right) \\ \end{pmatrix} \end{aligned}\]

            If I solve this Differential Equation Numerically, I have no problem getting solutions for an arbitrarily long time period, although if we were to do something like a binomial pulse then the pulse duration would have to be much smaller in order to generate points corresponding to different IC's.

            So for example using the following code I could solve for an entire 1 ms pulse, but because there are so many periods it isn't very useful:

(*length=α/ω;*)
BlochX=x'[t]==-R2 x[t]+γ (B0 y[t]-k x[t] z[t]+Brf z[t] Sin[ϕ+t ω]);
BlochY=y'[t]==-R2 y[t]+γ (-B0 x[t]-k y[t] z[t]+Brf z[t] Cos[ϕ+t ω]);
BlochZ=z'[t]==-R1 (-M0+z[t])+γ (k (x[t])^2+k (y[t])^2-Brf y[t] Cos[ϕ+t ω]-Brf x[t] Sin[ϕ+t ω]);

            By reducing this to a 0.1 $μ$s pulse the results become more meaningful:

            ##### Choosing a $k$-value

            For a pulse of 0.1 \(\mu\) s, I tried a variety of \(k\) values, I found that only \(k\) values from -50 to about 5 made any significant difference, this is shown in the animation here

            https://ryangreenup.github.io/NMR-Project/Working/RadiationDamping/RadiationDamping.html#laboratory-frame

        • Rotating Frame
          • Math

            So in the rotating Frame:

            \[\begin{aligned} \mathbf{B}&= \begin{pmatrix} B_{\mathrm{RF}}\cdot \cos\left( 0 \times t + \phi_{0} \right) - k\cdot M_y \\ B_{\mathrm{RF}}\cdot \sin\left( 0 \times t+ \phi_0 \right)+ k\cdot M_x \\ \Delta B_0 \\ \end{pmatrix} \\ &= \begin{pmatrix} B_{\mathrm{RF}}\cdot \cos\left(\phi_0 \right) - k\cdot M_y \\ B_{\mathrm{RF}}\cdot \sin\left( \phi_0\right)+ k\cdot M_x \\ \frac{\Omega}{\gamma} \\ \end{pmatrix} \\ \mathbf{M}&= \begin{pmatrix} M_x \\ M_y \\ M_z \\ \end{pmatrix} \end{aligned}\]

            and hence:

            \[\begin{aligned} \frac{\mathrm{d}\mathbf{M} }{\mathrm{d} t}&= M\times B - \mathbf{R}\left( \mathbf{M} - M_0 \right)\\ &= \begin{pmatrix} \gamma\left[ - k x z + \frac{y\Omega}{\gamma} + B_{\mathrm{RF}} z \sin\left( \phi \right) \right] - R_2 y \\ \gamma \left[ - k y z - \frac{x\Omega}{\gamma}+ B_{\mathrm{RF}} z \cos\left( \phi \right) \right] - R_2 y \\ \gamma\left[ k x^2 + k y^2t - B_{\mathrm{RF}} y \cos\left( \phi \right)- B_{\mathrm{RF}} x \sin\left( \phi \right) \right] - R_1\left( z - M_0 \right) \end{pmatrix} \end{aligned}\]

            This can just as easily be solved numerically and plotted
